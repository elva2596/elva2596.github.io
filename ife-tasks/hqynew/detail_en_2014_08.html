BANG, 2014

pottery clay

53pcs, ∅10cm-35cm

Sculptures are concerned with space. We usually present or convey space when we render an object (make a sculpture), whereas in Bang, I am directly allowing space (specifically air) to take part in the formation and shaping of the sculpture. I pinched clay into more or less a bowl shape and flung it with the opening of the bowl facing the wall. With a bang, the air exploded the clay into fragments, and what was left was the clay reshaped by the air. It’s the result of the clay directly responding to the air.
