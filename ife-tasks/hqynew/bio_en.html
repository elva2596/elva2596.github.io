Biography

Hu Qingyan (b. 1982, Weifang) currently lives and works in Beijing. As an important representative of the young artists, his works are well known to the people by exploring the possibility of sculpturing. He acquired steady skills in representational and realistic parts during the study in Guangzhou Academy of Fine Arts. His works has been more and more conceptualized since he finished the MA course in CAFA. He does not just focus on the perfect image molding, but also on exploring and expanding of the concept of sculpture and the language itself. The work is not only includes the traditional materials of sculpturing, such as wood, stone, metal, paper and ceramic, it includes the image, video and other media which records the process and performance in sculpturing. Nonetheless, “flow” and “transformation” is always the important topic of his sculptures.

There is more metal used in his recent practice, Hu Qingyan puts space (air) in the sculpture and tries to take the accidentalism and disorder into it as well. Swallowing up and rebuilding the space through the “hollow husk”, which makes the sculpture not only an external shape but more as a container and expanded space.

Hu Qingyan’s works have been exhibited in various museums and institutions around the world, such as Shut up and paint (National Gallery of Vitoria, Melbourne, Australia); The Exhibition of Annual of Contemporary Art of China (Beijing Minsheng Art Museum, Beijing, China ); China International Installation Art (Today Art Museum, Beijing, China ); 28 Chinese (Rubell Family Collection / Contemporary Arts Foundation, Miami, USA ) and M+ Sigg Collection: Four Decades of Chinese Contemporary Art (ArtisTree, Hong Kong, China). His works also collected by National Gallery of Victoria, Rubell Family Collection, K11 Art Foundation, M+ Sigg Collection, Today Art Museum and so on.
