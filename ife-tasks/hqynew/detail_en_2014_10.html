Fruitless Trees, 2014

cast bronze

29pcs, sizes vary from 11×21×10cm to 45.5×92×56cm

I received these tree-shaped bronze casts ready-made directly from a bronze foundry. They have been cut from the "channels" where the molten metal flows into the sculpture cast from above. If we can understand the bronze sculptures as the fruit of these “trees,” then the whole process becomes clear: a molten copper alloy, at more than 1,000 degrees, flows through a "funnel" and along the forked channels until it reaches the sculpture’s form. It flows from the root, to the trunk, and into the branches, finally reaching the tips, where it becomes the fruit. This is a perfect example of a space for growth and extension. It has the shape of a tree, but it’s not just a tree shape, rather it's more of an extended space for the idea of a tree. It's about the space of mere objects.
