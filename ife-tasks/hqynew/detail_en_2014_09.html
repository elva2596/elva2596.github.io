Mountain of Gold No.2, 2014

gold paper

(H)100×Ø230cm

It reminds one at first glance of Felix Gonzalez-Torres's piles of candy. But unlike Gonzalez-Torres's series, the small paper shapes are not gifts for the visitor, but just the opposite; they are commonly used as an offering. The artist folded when it is burned for the dead. This ritual is performed with the idea that the deceased can use the money in afterlife. Hence, the fake, symbolic money, which is not used in strict accordance with the ritual, is an illusion emblematic of wrong goals and the fictitiousness of currency.
