Electromotor, Lifting Jack, and Iron Cabinet, 2013

metal read-made objects (electromotor, lifting jack, and iron cabinet), welded metal

3 pieces, ∅23cm, ∅40cm, ∅100cm

Before they became iron spheres, they were three objects with completely different shapes and functions. Welding was started from a certain point on the motor and as the molten metal began to accumulate, the shape begins to get fatter. After welding tens of thousands of spots, the object's original form has completely gone and it has become unrecognizably an iron sphere. The other two objects are "grown" in the same way. These three objects that are completely unrelated in function and appearance are, in the end, no different except for their size. The process has erased their individuality, but highlighted their commonalities. It demonstrates the fluidity of function and form and the connection between unrelated objects.
